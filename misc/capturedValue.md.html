Capitalism remains a useful system, but one could spend lifetimes exploring the ways it breaks down, the edge cases, the coordination problems, the ways systems can get stuck in local maxima.

Much better authors than I have explored the topic. Personally I'd recommend Scott Alexander's "meditations on moloch".

The Goddess answers: “What is the matter with that, if it’s what you want to do?”

Malaclypse: “But nobody wants it! Everybody hates it!”

Goddess: “Oh. Well, then stop.”

The implicit question is – if everyone hates the current system, who perpetuates it? And Ginsberg answers: “Moloch”. It’s powerful not because it’s correct – nobody literally thinks an ancient Carthaginian demon causes everything – but because thinking of the system as an agent throws into relief the degree to which the system isn’t an agent.
